## Why Does Token Velocity Matter?

Most traders watch price. But price is the last thing to move. By the time a token's price spikes, the smart money has already positioned. Token velocity captures the three signals that precede price action:

### 1. Volume Acceleration

When a token's trading volume begins accelerating relative to its own baseline — not compared to other tokens, but relative to its own recent history — that's the earliest detectable signal of incoming momentum. BaseRadar measures this as the primary velocity factor.

### 2. Social Signal Density

On-chain data tells you what's happening. Social signals tell you why. When a token starts trending on X, showing up in developer discussions, or getting mentioned across crypto communities, that social density amplifies the volume signal. Today, BOTTOM G on Solana is trending #1 on X with high social velocity but minimal liquidity — a classic early-stage signal.

### 3. Liquidity Shifts

Liquidity doesn't lie. When market makers start positioning, when liquidity pools deepen, when bid-ask spreads tighten — these are institutional-grade signals. TAO BITTENSOR and CUBBON BLR on Base are holding steady volume, suggesting sustained interest rather than a pump-and-dump.

## The Trap Most Traders Fall Into

Picture this: you see a token up 40% on the day. You check the chart. It looks like it's breaking out. You buy in.

What you didn't see: the velocity score peaked 6 hours ago. Volume acceleration has been declining since the initial surge. Social mentions are dropping. Liquidity is thinning as early buyers take profit.

You just bought someone else's exit.

This is the fundamental problem with price-based analysis. Price is a lagging indicator. It tells you what already happened, not what's about to happen. Token velocity flips this — it measures the rate of change in the signals that drive price, giving you a leading indicator instead of a lagging one.
