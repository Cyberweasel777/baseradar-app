Solana is the noisiest chain we track. Hundreds of new tokens launch daily via PumpFun. Most fade within hours. A small number hit escape velocity.

That volatility makes Solana both the hardest chain to track manually and the most rewarding to track systematically. Velocity scoring cuts through the noise.

## How Solana Scores Different From Other Chains

Solana has unique scoring characteristics compared to Base and Ethereum:

**Higher velocity variance.** Because PumpFun launches so many tokens per day, the SURGE/FADING cycle is compressed. A token can go from 20 to 85 in 4 hours. On Ethereum, that same move might take days.

**Lower holder baseline.** New Solana tokens start with very few holders. Holder growth signals are therefore more sensitive — a small absolute number of new holders can move the score significantly early in a token's lifecycle.

**PumpFun graduation signal.** When a token graduates from PumpFun to a full DEX listing, velocity typically spikes. We treat this as a distinct signal layer within the Solana ecosystem score.

## What to Watch on Solana

**SURGE cluster events.** When 5+ Solana tokens hit SURGE simultaneously, it's a different signal than one token surging. Cluster events suggest a narrative is forming — meme theme, sector trend, or ecosystem catalyst.

**PumpFun graduation rate.** When the graduation rate (tokens surviving to full DEX listing) rises, it signals healthier market participation — buyers are holding through volatility.

**Holder growth velocity on new launches.** New tokens that reach 500+ unique holders within 24 hours have historically had 3x+ the survival rate of those that don't.

## Comparing Solana to Base

The most interesting comparison right now is [Solana vs Base](/compare/solana-vs-base).

Solana has more raw velocity events — more tokens, more movement. But Base has been showing higher *quality* velocity signals — fewer SURGE events but a higher percentage of them sustaining above 70 for multiple days.

High-frequency velocity (Solana) vs sustained velocity (Base) are two different signals. Neither is inherently superior — they reflect different market dynamics and different token lifecycles.
