## Why Base Is Different

Base is Coinbase's Ethereum L2. A few things make it structurally different from other chains from a velocity scoring perspective:

**Zora integration.** Base is the primary home for Zora's creator ecosystem — NFTs, creator coins, social tokens. Zora activity adds a completely different signal layer from pure DeFi/memecoin activity. Creator momentum is correlated with but distinct from trading momentum.

**Coinbase distribution.** Base tokens have a direct path to Coinbase retail distribution that other chains don't. When a Base token starts gaining velocity, the addressable retail audience is orders of magnitude larger than a comparable Solana token.

**Lower noise floor.** Fewer tokens launch on Base per day compared to Solana. This means the SURGE signals we see on Base have a higher signal-to-noise ratio — when something surges on Base, it's more likely to be organic.

## Base vs Ethereum on Velocity

Base and Ethereum share security (Base settles to Ethereum) but diverge sharply on velocity characteristics.

Ethereum's velocity scores are more stable and mean-reverting. Large DeFi protocols dominate the signal — they don't spike dramatically. Ethereum is a store of value and settlement layer, and the velocity data reflects that.

Base is where the growth is happening. New protocols, new creators, new token launches. Higher velocity ceiling, higher variance, higher opportunity surface for velocity-based signals.
